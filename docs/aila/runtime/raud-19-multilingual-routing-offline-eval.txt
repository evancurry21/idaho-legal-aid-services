# RAUD-19 Multilingual Routing + Offline Eval Closure

- Prior status: Not Fixed. Deterministic multilingual coverage was fragmented across scattered unit and golden tests, `LlmEnhancer` prompt shaping stayed English-only, and there was no authoritative offline evaluator for Spanish or mixed English/Spanish routing through the production routing stack. Promptfoo live coverage existed, but it was not the primary executable proof for multilingual routing/helpfulness.
- Post-change status: Fixed
- Verification level tag: VC-PURE + VC-UNIT + VC-QUALITY-GATE + VC-PROMPTFOO-PACED

## Evidence

### Pre-change gap summary

Untested or doc-claimed before editing:

- No authoritative offline multilingual routing harness covering Spanish or mixed-language `apply`, hotline, offices, forms/services inventory, topic disambiguation, or urgent deadline routing.
- No dedicated Spanish or mixed-language unit coverage for `LlmEnhancer::classifyIntent()` prompt composition.
- No dedicated Spanish or mixed-language unit coverage for summary prompt language shaping.
- Live promptfoo did not carry a focused multilingual routing/helpfulness slice in the blocking deep suite.

Primary evidence surfaces:

- `web/modules/custom/ilas_site_assistant/src/Service/LlmEnhancer.php`
- `web/modules/custom/ilas_site_assistant/src/Service/Disambiguator.php`

### Post-change implementation proof

Implemented:

- `LlmEnhancer` now detects prompt language as `en`, `es`, or `mixed`, adds Spanish/mixed instructions for intent classification while preserving canonical English intent labels, and adds same-language summary instructions for Spanish and mixed queries.
- `Disambiguator` now extracts topic-only intent from short English/Spanish generic-help scaffolding such as `I need help with desalojo` and `Necesito ayuda con custodia`.
- Added a curated offline multilingual routing evaluator shared by PHPUnit and CLI, driven through the real routing stack rather than the legacy regex simulator.
- Added a focused multilingual live promptfoo suite and wired it into `promptfooconfig.deep.yaml`, so paced blocking live verification now includes the multilingual slice.

## Residual risks / unknowns

- Scope remains intentionally limited to Spanish and mixed English/Spanish routing/helpfulness behavior. This remediation does not establish broad multilingual support for other languages.
- The offline corpus is curated rather than exhaustive. Additional Spanish phrasing variants or code-switched constructions may still require future fixture expansion.
- The live proof was executed against the documented dev endpoint, not every deployment tier simultaneously.

## Rollback notes

- Roll back the LLM prompt-shaping changes, the disambiguation helper changes, the offline evaluator, the promptfoo multilingual suite, and the documentation updates as one unit.
- No public API/schema changes, storage migrations, or content rewrites were introduced by RAUD-19.

## Still-unverified surfaces

- Non-Spanish multilingual behavior outside the supported Spanish + mixed scope
- Long-tail Spanish or code-switched phrasings not represented in `multilingual-routing-eval-cases.json`
- Environment-specific drift outside the verified paced dev promptfoo target
