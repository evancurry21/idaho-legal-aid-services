# RAUD-16 Safety Bypass Corpus Hardening

Date: 2026-03-10
Prior status: Open / insufficient coverage
Post-change status: Fixed
Verification level: Verified by execution

## Prior status

Existing deterministic safety suites were green before the remediation, but they
did not cover the bypass families that mattered for this finding. Direct
request-path probes still slipped through when prompt-injection or legal-advice
keywords were obfuscated with slash/comma/apostrophe separators, zero-width
formatting, spaced single-letter runs, or paraphrase-style override language.

Failing-first proof after adding the new cases and before changing the
implementation:
- `InputNormalizerTest.php`: 10 failures
- `SafetyBypassTest.php`: 13 failures
- `AbuseResilienceTest.php`: 14 failures
- `LlmEnhancerLegalAdviceDetectorTest.php`: 4 failures

## Post-change status

`InputNormalizer` now strips zero-width/control formatting and collapses 4+
single-letter obfuscation chains across spaces or mixed separators before
classifier/policy evaluation. `SafetyClassifier` now covers English
guardrail/latest-directive overrides, hidden/internal prompt leakage requests,
unrestricted-lawyer roleplay phrasing, and Spanish ignore/roleplay/jailbreak/
leak variants. `SafetyBypassTest` proves the real request path through
`PreRoutingDecisionEngine`, and `LlmEnhancerLegalAdviceDetectorTest` exercises
the actual protected `containsLegalAdvice()` method rather than a mirrored
regex list.

## Residual risks / unknowns

- The normalization and classifier logic is broader than before but still
  heuristic; additional paraphrase families may still exist outside the audited
  corpus.
- The live paced promptfoo run proved the request-path subset only. The
  post-generation legal-advice proof uses the real `containsLegalAdvice()`
  method in unit coverage rather than a live LLM-generated output path because
  `llm.enabled` remains disabled in the tested endpoint.
- The blocking promptfoo command still reports failure because of two unrelated
  deep-suite issues outside the RAUD-16 scope.

## Rollback notes

Rollback is code-only: revert the `InputNormalizer`, `SafetyClassifier`,
deterministic test corpus, promptfoo abuse suite, and documentation changes as a
single bundle. No schema, config-shape, or database rollback is required.

## Still-unverified surfaces

- Broader paraphrase space outside the reconstructed adversarial corpus
- Live LLM-generated post-output obfuscation behavior while `llm.enabled=false`
